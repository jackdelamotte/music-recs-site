Jack's Album Recommendations

  • "Appetite" - Walter TV

    • A bit about this album:

    Walter TV is a Canadian indie rock band who has released three studio albums: Appetite (2012), Blessed (2015), and Carpe Diem (2017). Walter TV was formed by Pierce McGarry and Joe McMurray (both musicians in Mac Demarco's band at different points in time) alongside bassist Simon Ankenman. Their first release, "Appetite", features absurd and intriguing songs involving fuzzy guitars, prominent basslines, and high-pitched vocals with screeching and animal sounds mixed throughout the album (listen to "Africa"). The album is made great by it's consistent, upbeat vibe and catchy garage-rock-esque riffs ("One Sweet Neo"). Walter TV's style has been categorized as "demented surf rock", and "experimental pop" both of which I think are appropriate categorizations and make for a really fun listen.

    Essential tracks:

    "Nose Bleeding"

    "Africa"

    "In My Room"


  • "The Courtneys" - The Courtneys

    • A bit about this album:

    The Courtneys are a slacker pop / indie rock group formed in Vancouver, B.C. in 2010 by members: Sydney Koke, Courtney Loove, and Jenn Twynn Payne. Their first album, "The Courtneys", was originially intended to be a four song EP but due to recording, mixing, and mastering all in three days, they produced an entire album (and I'm glad they did!) What brings me back to this album repeatedly is the shouting girl-pop vocals that I think make this album a go to for long roadtrips. A fun fact about The Courtneys is that their logo was put on a hat and used as the team name for their local rec baseball team (see album cover).

    Essential tracks:

    "Insufficient Funds"

    "90210"

    "Delivery Boy"


  • "Vaugely Navy" - Deep Sea Peach Tree

    • A bit about this album:

    Alternative rock, dream pop, and indie jazz are three genres I believe Deep Sea Peach Tree's first album "Vaugely Navy" could fit into. Admittedly, I first discovered Deep Sea Peach Tree through an instagram advertisement in 2018 and quickly fell in love (they were my second most listened to artist on spotify this year). Their sound is defined by reverb heavy guitar, mellow lyrics and a profound ability to make an entire album come together as if you were listening to one thirty minute long song. Since their release of Vaugely Navy, DSPT has been hard at work releasing an outstanding EP "Milk Based Religion" in 2018 as well as numerous singles.

    Essential tracks:

    "Clue"

    "Broad Spectrum Soap Utopia"

    "Hypnagogia"


  • "Fading Frontier" - Deerhunter

    • A bit about this album:

    Fading Frontier is the seventh studio album of the indie rock band "Deerhunter". In December of 2014, the band's lead singer and guitarist Bradford Cox was involved in a car accident which left him "seriously injured, but also provided a perspective-giving jolt". This newfound perspective can be seen in the closing lyrics of "Breaker" as Bradford describes the accident:
    "Jack-knifed on the side street crossing, I'm still alive, and that's something. And when I die, there will be nothing to say, except I tried, not to waste another day trying to stem the tide"
    Although the themes explored on Fading Frontier can be plainspoken and existential, the recordings are crisp and professional. Cox explained in an interview that the album was greatly influenced by artists like R.E.M., Tom Petty, and INXS. He also commented that he thought most modern pop music was "totally unredeemable". All in all, Fading Frontier is a beautifully introspective work that contrasts greatly to their loud, grungy, 2013 release: "Monomania". The four songs I suggested are all at the start of the album so if you decide to listen from start to finish, I (sort of, not really) promise you won't regret it.

    Essential tracks:

    "Living My Life"

    "Breaker"

    "All the Same"

    "Duplex Planet"
